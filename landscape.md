## 4. 20VC Pricing Playbook — The Quant Layer

The README states the SaaS Massacre framing, the $4B/50% IPO threshold, and the 10–15x FCF floor. The auxiliary quant material:

**Growth-tier multiples (NTM revenue):**

| Growth | Multiple |
|---|---|
| <20% | 3–5x |
| 20–30% | ~12x |
| 30%+ | ~24x |

Hidden variable: ~half of "15% growth" is price increases on existing customers, not new logos — moves companies between tiers.

**The Fortnite Effect (five mechanisms by which foundation models compress incumbent surface area):**
1. Customer "maiming" — seat compression without churn
2. TAM exhaustion
3. COVID demand reversion
4. Horizontal category convergence
5. Pricing pressure toward commoditization

**Public SaaS bloodbath, by ticker (Q1 2026 YTD):**

| Ticker | YTD |
|---|---|
| Atlassian | –67% |
| Shopify | –25% |
| Gartner | –71% |

**Series B base rate (Carta — 547 deals, 2018 vintage):**

| Outcome | Share |
|---|---|
| <1x | 35% |
| 1–5x | 50% |
| >5x | 18% |
| 100x (Figma alone) | <1% |

Two-thirds of all Series B investments return <2x.

**TAM exhaustion rule:** At $100M ARR, ≤1% penetration = runway, 1–2% = caution, ≥5% = headwall within 2–3 years. **Toast at $22B is the ceiling for pure vertical SaaS.** Test for any vertical AI bet: "will this be bigger than Toast?"

**Growth decay curve:** Each year's growth ≈ 85% of prior. 60% → ~51% → ~43%. ~1-in-3 re-accelerate after one year of deceleration; ~1-in-9–10 manage two consecutive years.

**NRR compression benchmark:** Databricks at 150% NRR on $5B ARR = healthy. Companies approaching 100% NRR are retreating. SMB churns fastest; ServiceNow's 99% GRR with 5-year contracts is the enterprise ceiling.

**Pricing-economics ladder (replaces, doesn't augments, the README's outcome-pricing note):**

| Product | Price |
|---|---|
| Legacy per-seat SaaS | $8–12 / seat |
| Canva | $8 / month |
| Gamma | $100 / month |
| Cursor | $500 / month |
| Agentic deployment | $50–100K / deployment |

**Risk:** when 2–3 competitors emerge in the same agentic category, premium pricing erodes — $1,000 labour savings splits across 3 providers at ~$100 each.

**Entry-price-vs-TAM exemplars (named comps):**
- Cursor $29B — rational; developer-tool TAM proven.
- Harvey $8B — risk is TAM validation.
- Sierra $10B — passes category + team boxes; entry-price risk.
- Anthropic $380B (Feb 2026) — "cheaper than last round on forward revenue."

**Category-king efficiency benchmarks:**
- Palantir runs a **Rule of 94** with 10% fewer employees.
- HubSpot shows **2.8x better efficiency** than 2021.

**Five stress-test questions for any late-stage valuation:**
1. Growth net of price increases?
2. NRR net of seat compression?
3. At $100M ARR, what TAM % captured?
4. If SaaS bedrock reverts to 6–7x NTM, fair value?
5. Did recent investors pay an access premium or illiquidity discount?

**Compression sequence to terminal value:** 20x revenue (hypergrowth) → 7–8x revenue (mature growth) → 12x FCF (cash flow maturity).

**The 2021 hangover, structural inversion:** Private illiquidity discount used to be 20–30%; now inverted to a **20–30% access premium**. Best companies avoid IPO via secondary liquidity.

## 5. The Liquidity Event Horizon — Naming the Layers

The README uses "Liquidity Paradox / Bifurcation" as a mental model. Auxiliary structural layers from the Cognitive Industrial Revolution synthesis:

**Five-layer stack** (each layer captures value by cannibalising adjacent ones):

> Energy → Chips → Hyperscalers → Foundation Models → Applications

Where "power is the ultimate veto."

**Two named 2026 hotspots** (terminology to use unprompted with the persona):

- **Hotspot 1 — Agent-native infrastructure.** As foundation labs absorb vertical tasks into base APIs ("**Intelligence Deflation**"), the application layer risks becoming "thin UI wrappers." Orchestration / routing layers become strategically critical.
- **Hotspot 2 — Multiplayer vertical AI.** Survival requires **Contextual Superiority** via proprietary feedback loops + regulatory knowledge — what the synthesis calls "**Semantic Moats**."

## 6. The Innermost Loop — Specifics the README Doesn't Carry

The README states the $660–690B hyperscaler capex and Brockman's GPU-shortage line. Auxiliary signal from Diamandis EP 254:

**Scale, in alternate units:** ~**$805B / year, ~$3B / day** across the broader industry (vs. the five-hyperscaler figure in README).

**Three-bottleneck nested constraint (terminology to use):**
1. **Chips & wafer starts.** Only TSMC, Samsung, Intel are advanced-fab capable. Musk has locked up 16B (potentially 45B) units of Samsung capacity. Jensen Huang has no long-term TSMC agreement.
2. **Energy & permitting.** Microsoft has GPUs it cannot deploy due to insufficient powered land. China and UAE deploy capacity faster than US regulation permits.
3. **Per-token economic productivity.** Compute flows to the highest-revenue tokens first.

**Geographic redistribution of US data centers:**
- 67% of planned DCs are in rural areas.
- 48% target the South; 39% in counties with no existing DC infrastructure.
- Diamandis frame: "the biggest geographic wealth transfer since fracking."

**New asset classes** (the "exotic two years ago" categories the README alludes to):

- **Panthalassa** — wave-powered, seawater-cooled ocean data centers. **$140M at $1B valuation**, commercial deployment targeted 2027. Viable because Starlink eliminates undersea-cable dependency.
- **StarCloud** — orbital data centers. **$200M at $2.2B valuation**, one month after a $1.1B round. Plan: 88,000 LEO satellites; first H100 GPU launched 2025. Critical dependency: launch-provider access (SpaceX, Blue Origin, Relativity).

**Infrastructure re-rating (named comps for any compute-adjacent pitch):**
