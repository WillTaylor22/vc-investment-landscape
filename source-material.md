## 3. 20VC Pricing Playbook — Quant Framework

### Growth-tier multiples (NTM revenue)

| Growth Rate | Multiple |
|---|---|
| <20% | 3–5x |
| 20–30% | ~12x |
| 30%+ | ~24x |

**Hidden variable:** ~half of reported "15% growth" comes from price increases on existing customers, not new logos.

### Series B base rate (Carta — 547 deals, 2018 vintage)

| Outcome | Share |
|---|---|
| <1x | 35% |
| 1–5x | 50% |
| >5x | 18% |
| 100x (Figma alone) | <1% |

Two-thirds of all Series B investments return less than 2x.

### TAM exhaustion rule

At $100M ARR: ≤1% penetration → runway. 1–2% → caution. ≥5% → headwall within 2–3 years. Toast at $22B is the ceiling for pure vertical SaaS — the "will this be bigger than Toast?" test.

### Growth decay curve

Each year's growth ≈ 85% of prior. 60% → ~51% → ~43%. Only ~1-in-3 re-accelerate after one year of deceleration; ~1-in-9–10 manage two consecutive years.

### NRR compression pattern

Healthy benchmark: Databricks 150% NRR on $5B ARR. Watchword: "at every renewal conversation, customers only want 90% of last year's seats." SMB churns fastest; enterprise slowest (ServiceNow holds 99% GRR with 5-year contracts).

### Public SaaS Massacre (Q1 2026)

| Ticker | YTD |
|---|---|
| Atlassian | -67% |
| Shopify | -25% |
| Gartner | -71% |

O'Driscoll: "Every single quarter since Q1 2022, growth has declined across all public SaaS stocks."

### Pricing economics shift

| Tool | Price |
|---|---|
| Legacy per-seat SaaS | $8–12 / seat |
| Canva (legacy creative) | $8 / month |
| Gamma (AI-era) | $100 / month |
| Cursor (value-based dev) | $500 / month |
| Agentic deployment (replaces headcount) | $50–100K / deployment |

### IPO threshold (early 2026)

- New bar: **$4B ARR + 50% growth**.
- Every 2025 IPO was a down round.
- Terminal anchor for mature software: **~12x FCF** (10–15x is the floor for ex-growth SaaS).
- Compression sequence: 20x revenue → 7–8x revenue → 12x FCF.

### Five questions for stress-testing any valuation

1. Growth net of price increases?
2. NRR net of seat compression?
3. At $100M ARR, what TAM % captured?
4. If SaaS bedrock reverts to 6–7x NTM, what's fair value?
5. Did recent investors pay an access premium or an illiquidity discount?

## 4. The Innermost Loop — Specifics

**Hyperscaler capex.** Five-hyperscaler 2026 commitments: **$660–690B** (Goldman models up to $200B upside, putting 2027 above $1T). Per Diamandis EP 254: industry total tracking to **~$805B / year (~$3B / day)**.

**The three nested bottlenecks:**
1. **Chips & wafer starts.** TSMC, Samsung, Intel are the only three advanced-fab capable. Musk locked up 16B (potentially 45B) units of Samsung. Jensen Huang has no long-term TSMC agreement.
2. **Energy & permitting.** Microsoft owns GPUs it cannot deploy due to lack of powered land. China and UAE deploy capacity faster than US regulation permits.
3. **Per-token economic productivity.** Compute flows to the highest-revenue tokens first.

**Geographic redistribution:**
- 67% of planned US data centers are in rural areas
- 48% target the South; 39% in counties with no existing DC infrastructure
- Diamandis: "the biggest geographic wealth transfer since fracking"

**New asset classes:**
- **Panthalassa** — wave-powered, seawater-cooled ocean data centers. **$140M at $1B valuation.** Commercial deployment targeted 2027. Viable because Starlink replaces undersea cables.
- **StarCloud** — orbital data centers. **$200M at $2.2B valuation** (one month after a $1.1B round). Plan: 88,000 satellites in LEO; first H100 GPU launched 2025.

**PE as the AI deployment channel.** OpenAI partnered with TPG / Brookfield / Advent ($10B). Anthropic partnered with Blackstone / Goldman / Hellman & Friedman ($1.5B). Salem Ismail: "the organizational singularity."

## 6. The Liquidity Paradox — Two 2026 Hotspots

From the Cognitive Industrial Revolution synthesis (Alpha-matica):

**Five-layer industrial stack:** Energy → Chips → Hyperscalers → Foundation Models → Applications.

**Hotspot 1 — Agent-native infrastructure.** Foundation labs are absorbing vertical tasks into base APIs ("Intelligence Deflation"), turning the application layer into "thin UI wrappers." Orchestration / routing layers become strategically critical.

**Hotspot 2 — Multiplayer vertical AI.** Survival via "Contextual Superiority" — proprietary feedback loops + regulatory data + workflow depth. The counter-bet to horizontal absorption.
